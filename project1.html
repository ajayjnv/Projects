Chicken Dum Biryani


Ingredients


For Marination:

  • Chicken (500g, cut into medium pieces)
  • Yogurt (½ cup)
  • Ginger-garlic paste (1 tablespoon)
  • Red chili powder (1 teaspoon)
  • Turmeric powder (½ teaspoon)
  • Garam masala (1 teaspoon)
  • Coriander powder (1 teaspoon)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Lemon juice (1 tablespoon)
  • Fried onions (Birista, ¼ cup)
  • Green chilies (2-3, slit)

For Rice:

  • Basmati rice (2 cups)
  • Water (as needed for boiling)
  • Whole spices: bay leaf, cloves, cardamom, cinnamon stick (for aromatic rice)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Ghee or oil (1 teaspoon, optional for fragrance)

For Dum and Layering:

  • Saffron strands (a pinch, soaked in 2 tablespoons of warm milk)
  • Ghee (2 tablespoons)
  • Fried onions (extra for layering)
  • Mint leaves (fresh, a handful)
  • Coriander leaves (fresh, chopped, a handful)

Chicken Dum Biryani Procedure


Step 1: Marination

In a large bowl, mix the chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, coriander powder, salt, lemon juice, fried onions, and slit green chilies. Cover the bowl and marinate the chicken for at least 2 hours (overnight for best results).


Step 2: Preparing the Rice

Wash the basmati rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 30 minutes. In a large pot, boil water and add whole spices (bay leaf, cloves, cardamom, cinnamon stick) and salt. Add the soaked rice and cook until it is 70% cooked (parboiled). Drain the rice and set it aside.


Step 3: Layering the Biryani

In a heavy-bottomed pot, spread a layer of marinated chicken at the bottom. Spread a layer of parboiled rice over the chicken. Sprinkle fried onions, chopped mint leaves, coriander leaves, and a few drops of saffron milk over the rice layer. Repeat the layers if needed, ending with a rice layer on top. Drizzle ghee over the top layer for added flavor.


Step 4: Dum Cooking

Seal the pot tightly with a lid. You can use dough to ensure a proper seal. Place the pot on low heat and cook for 25-30 minutes to allow the flavors to blend (dum cooking). Alternatively, you can place the sealed pot on a heated griddle (tava) for indirect heat and cook for the same duration.


Step 5: Serving

Carefully open the lid and mix the biryani gently to avoid breaking the rice grains. Serve hot with raita, salad, or your favorite side dish.


Puri - Potato Curry


Ingredients


For Puri:

  • Whole wheat flour (2 cups)
  • Salt (½ teaspoon)
  • Oil or ghee (1 tablespoon)
  • Water (as needed for kneading)
  • Oil (for deep frying)

For Potato Curry:

  • Potatoes (4-5, boiled and diced)
  • Onions (2, finely chopped)
  • Tomatoes (2, finely chopped)
  • Green chilies (2, slit)
  • Ginger-garlic paste (1 teaspoon)
  • Turmeric powder (½ teaspoon)
  • Red chili powder (1 teaspoon)
  • Coriander powder (1 teaspoon)
  • Garam masala (½ teaspoon)
  • Mustard seeds (½ teaspoon)
  • Curry leaves (a few, optional)
  • Oil (2 tablespoons)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Water (as needed for the curry)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Puri and Potato Curry Procedure :


Step 1: Preparing the Puri Dough

In a large bowl, mix the whole wheat flour and salt. Add a tablespoon of oil or ghee and rub it into the flour. Gradually add water to form a stiff dough. Knead the dough until smooth and elastic, then cover it with a damp cloth. Let it rest for about 20 minutes.


Step 2: Rolling the Puris

After resting, divide the dough into small equal-sized balls. Roll each ball into a small circle (about 3-4 inches in diameter) using a rolling pin. Make sure the puris are evenly rolled and not too thick.


Step 3: Frying the Puris

Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, gently slide in the rolled puris, one at a time. Fry them until they puff up and turn golden brown on both sides. Remove them from the oil and drain excess oil on a paper towel. Continue frying the remaining puris.


Step 4: Preparing the Potato Curry

Heat oil in a pan, and add mustard seeds. When they splutter, add curry leaves (if using) and green chilies. Add chopped onions and sauté them until they become golden brown. Then add ginger-garlic paste and cook for another minute. Stir in chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften.


Step 5: Cooking the Curry

Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt to the tomatoes. Stir the mixture well and let it cook for a minute. Add the boiled and diced potatoes and mix gently. You can mash some of the potatoes to thicken the curry. Pour in water to adjust the consistency, and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes. Finally, add garam masala and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.


Step 6: Serving

Serve the hot puris with the potato curry. Enjoy this delicious Indian meal with yogurt or pickle, if desired!


South Indian Dosa


Ingredients


For Dosa Batter:

  • Rice (2 cups, preferably parboiled rice)
  • Urad dal (black gram, ½ cup)
  • Fenugreek seeds (1 teaspoon)
  • Poha (flattened rice, 2 tablespoons, optional)
  • Water (as needed for soaking and grinding)
  • Salt (to taste)

For Cooking Dosas:

  • Oil or ghee (for greasing the pan)
  • Optional toppings: finely chopped onions, green chilies, or coriander leaves

South Indian Dosa Procedure :


Step 1: Preparing the Batter

Wash the rice and urad dal thoroughly in water. Soak the rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds separately for 4-6 hours. If using poha, soak it for 30 minutes before grinding. After soaking, drain the water and grind the urad dal and fenugreek seeds into a smooth paste, adding water as needed. Grind the rice and poha (if used) into a slightly coarse paste. Combine the two pastes in a large bowl and mix well. Cover the bowl and allow the batter to ferment overnight or for 8-12 hours in a warm place.


Step 2: Preparing the Batter for Cooking

After fermentation, the batter should have risen and turned slightly frothy. Add salt to the batter and mix gently. Adjust the consistency with water to make it pourable but not too runny.


Step 3: Cooking the Dosas

Heat a flat, non-stick pan or a cast-iron griddle over medium heat. Once hot, lightly grease the pan with oil or ghee using a paper towel or a piece of onion. Pour a ladleful of batter in the center of the pan and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin layer. Drizzle a few drops of oil or ghee around the edges of the dosa. Cook for 1-2 minutes until the edges start lifting and the dosa turns golden brown. Flip the dosa if you prefer cooking the other side; otherwise, serve it directly from the pan.


Step 4: Serving

Serve the hot dosas with coconut chutney, sambar, or any preferred side dish. Enjoy this classic South Indian delicacy!


Tamarind Rice


Ingredients


For Tamarind Rice:

  • Rice (1 cup)
  • Tamarind (small lemon-sized ball)
  • Jaggery (1-2 tablespoons, optional)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Turmeric powder (½ teaspoon)

For Tempering:

  • Oil (2 tablespoons)
  • Mustard seeds (1 teaspoon)
  • Cumin seeds (1 teaspoon)
  • Dry red chilies (2-3)
  • Curry leaves (a few)
  • Asafoetida (a pinch)

For Garnishing:

  • Roasted peanuts (2 tablespoons)
  • Roasted cashews (2 tablespoons)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Tamarind Rice Procedure :


Step 1: Preparing the Tamarind Paste

Soak a small piece of tamarind in warm water for about 10-15 minutes. After soaking, mash the tamarind and extract the juice by filtering out the seeds and fiber. Set the tamarind extract aside.


Step 2: Preparing the Rice

Cook the rice separately in a pressure cooker or on the stove until soft and fully cooked. Once cooked, spread the rice on a plate to let it cool slightly and avoid clumping.


Step 3: Tempering the Spices

Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds. Once they splutter, add cumin seeds, dry red chilies, and curry leaves. Sauté for a few seconds, then add a pinch of asafoetida (hing) for flavor.


Step 4: Adding the Tamarind Paste

Next, add the tamarind extract to the pan along with jaggery (if used), salt, and turmeric powder. Stir the mixture and cook it for 5-7 minutes, allowing the flavors to combine and the tamarind paste to thicken slightly.


Step 5: Mixing the Rice

Add the cooled rice to the tamarind mixture and mix well, ensuring the rice is evenly coated with the tamarind sauce. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally to combine the flavors.


Step 6: Garnishing and Serving

Garnish the tamarind rice with roasted peanuts, cashews, and fresh coriander leaves. Serve the tamarind rice hot, accompanied by papad, raita, or any side dish of your choice!
